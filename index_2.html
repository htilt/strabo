Crystal Springs

Here, water bubbles up from the ground over an one acre area and begins its journey through the Johnson Creek watershed. Filtered by clayey soils, this water is some of the cleanest in Portland.

Reed Lake

The water that pools in Reed Lake provides a habitat for a variety of native fish, turtles, and water fowl. A layer of loose silt disguises the six-foot depth of the lake bottom.

Fish Ladder

Built in 2001, the fish ladder connects Reed Lake with the rest of Crystal Springs. The ladder allows salmon fry, hatched in the stream, to swim up to Reed Lake until they mature.

The Farm

Acquired in 2007, the former Rivelli farm property now appears undeveloped. The property underwent major restoration under Reed's ownership to eliminate the culvert through which Crystal Springs once ran and to construct a natural creek bottom.
